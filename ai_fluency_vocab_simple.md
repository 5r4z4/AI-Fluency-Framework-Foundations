# AI Fluency Vocabulary — Simple Study Guide

Each term has a **one-line simple meaning** + a **memory hook**. Read top to bottom, or use it like flashcards (cover the right side and guess).

---

## 🧠 The Big Idea

| Term | Simple Meaning |
|---|---|
| **AI Fluency** | Being good, safe, and smart at working *with* AI. |
| **The 4Ds** | The 4 skills of AI Fluency: **D**elegation, **D**escription, **D**iscernment, **D**iligence. |

---

## 1️⃣ Delegation — "Who does what?"
> Deciding which parts of a task YOU do vs. which parts AI does.

| Term | Simple Meaning |
|---|---|
| Problem Awareness | Know your goal before you ask AI for help. |
| Platform Awareness | Know what this AI tool is good/bad at. |
| Task Delegation | Split the work smartly between you and AI. |

---

## 2️⃣ Description — "How do I talk to AI?"
> Telling AI clearly what you want and how to act.

| Term | Simple Meaning |
|---|---|
| Product Description | What you want the OUTPUT to look like (format, length, style). |
| Process Description | HOW the AI should work (step-by-step instructions). |
| Performance Description | HOW the AI should behave/act (short vs detailed, friendly vs strict). |

💡 Memory hook: **Product = the thing**, **Process = the steps**, **Performance = the personality**.

---

## 3️⃣ Discernment — "Is this AI answer any good?"
> Checking and judging AI's work critically.

| Term | Simple Meaning |
|---|---|
| Product Discernment | Is the OUTPUT accurate and good? |
| Process Discernment | Did the AI THINK correctly to get there? |
| Performance Discernment | Did the AI BEHAVE well while working with me? |

💡 Same 3-way split as Description — just checking instead of asking.

---

## 4️⃣ Diligence — "Am I being responsible?"
> Using AI ethically and owning the results.

| Term | Simple Meaning |
|---|---|
| Creation Diligence | Pick your AI tools carefully and use them thoughtfully. |
| Transparency Diligence | Be honest that AI helped you. |
| Deployment Diligence | Double-check the output before sharing/using it — it's on YOU. |

---

## 🤝 How Humans + AI Work Together

| Term | Simple Meaning |
|---|---|
| Automation | You give instructions → AI just does it. (no back-and-forth) |
| Augmentation | You and AI go back-and-forth together, like teammates. |
| Agency | You set up AI to work on its own, even talking to others for you. |

💡 Think of it as a scale: **Automation** (AI = tool) → **Augmentation** (AI = partner) → **Agency** (AI = agent acting for you).

---

## ⚙️ How AI Actually Works (Tech Basics)

| Term | Simple Meaning |
|---|---|
| Generative AI | AI that CREATES new stuff (text, images, code). |
| LLM (Large Language Model) | AI trained on tons of text to understand & write language. |
| Claude | Anthropic's AI model family. |
| Parameters | The "settings" inside the AI's brain — billions of them. |
| Neural Network | The AI's brain structure, inspired by real brains. |
| Transformer | The 2017 design that lets AI read whole sentences at once (not word-by-word). |
| Scaling Laws | Bigger AI + more data = better AI (usually). |
| Pre-training | Step 1: AI reads a massive pile of text to learn language basics. |
| Fine-tuning | Step 2: AI gets trained more to be helpful & safe. |
| Context Window | How much text AI can "remember" at once in a conversation. |
| Hallucination | AI confidently says something FALSE. |
| Knowledge Cutoff | The date after which AI knows nothing new. |
| Reasoning Model | AI that thinks step-by-step before answering. |
| Temperature | Randomness dial — high = creative/wild, low = safe/predictable. |
| RAG | AI looks stuff up externally instead of just guessing. |
| Bias | AI unfairly favoring certain groups/ideas (from its training data). |

---

## ✍️ Prompt Engineering (Talking to AI Well)

| Term | Simple Meaning |
|---|---|
| Prompt | Whatever you type/give the AI. |
| Prompt Engineering | The skill of writing GOOD prompts. |
| Chain-of-Thought | Asking AI to explain its steps, not just the answer. |
| Few-Shot Learning | Give AI examples so it copies the pattern. |
| Role/Persona | Tell AI to "act as" someone (e.g. "act like a lawyer"). |
| Output Constraints | Tell AI exactly the format/length you want. |
| Think-First Approach | Ask AI to think BEFORE answering, not just blurt it out. |
